Petrol Price and Consumption Worldwide

Petrol prices are notably higher in Europe, this is due to high taxes and environmental regulations
aimed at reducing carbon emissions. Middle Eastern countries such as Saudi Arabia and United Arab Emirates
have significantly lower petrol prices due to subsidies and government control over oil production.
Petrol price in North Korea is the highest in the world, at $14.5 per liter.




Asia contributes the most to global daily oil consumption because of its enormous population and growing economies. Oceania has the lowest daily oil consumption as the region has a relatively lower population. The United States of America has the highest daily oil consumption among all countries.




African countries generally have low to moderate petrol prices, lower GDP per capita and daily oil consumption.
